About

I started in writing and editing, spent nearly a decade in mixed-methods health services research at UW–Madison beginning in 2009,I keep a small grid that plots this arc, professional and personal, against the weeks of a single life: life in weeks. and moved into data engineering when I realized the tools I needed to study complex healthcare systems didn't exist yet.

The methodological thread runs from grounded theoryGrounded theory, developed by Glaser and Strauss in 1967, builds theory inductively from coded qualitative data rather than testing hypotheses against it. In practice this means reading transcripts, labeling passages, comparing labels across transcripts, and letting structure emerge. in qualitative research, through logistic and linear regression in population health, through time series analysis in Stars forecasting. Different instruments, the same underlying question about how complex systems behave under measurement. Right now that means Medicare Star Ratings, HEDIS pipelines, and data integration across claims and eligibility sources.Current role: Manager of Data Science & Engineering at Baltimore Health Analytics, Madison WI.

Experience

Manager, Data Science and Engineering

Baltimore Health Analytics · remote · team of two data scientists · Nov 2025 to Present

A Medicare Advantage quality platform anchors the work: Part C and Part D Star Ratings measures built on claims, eligibility, and pharmacy data. My remit is the methodology and engineering function behind it. That spans direct management and code review, data science and QA coordination across two geographies, and translation from CMS requirements into organizational priorities.

More detail

CMS Star Rating methodology is published in Technical Notes that run to hundreds of pages: regulatory language describing measure construction, denominator logic, significance testing, and improvement score calculation.The CMS Technical Notes are revised each rating year. A single measure rewrite can propagate into denominator changes, hold-harmless logic, and cut-point regeneration, all of which need to round-trip through the analytics layer without drift. The work translates that regulatory text into executable Python and SQL: pandas for data transformation, scipy and numpy for statistical components (including robust exponential smoothing for time series forecasting), and browser automation for source extraction. Every threshold and weighted average stays traceable to its source.

ψk(r) = rif |r| ≤ k k · sign(r)if |r| > k
Huber's ψ-function. I prototyped this robustification as a proof of concept, clipping large residuals so a single COVID-era shock would not dominate the smoothing parameter estimates. A tested proposal, not a deployed customer-analytics component.

HEDIS hybrid measures like Transitions of Care, along with the Part D medication adherence triplet (MAH, MAC, MAD), add a separate class of problem. The denominator is constructed from claims and eligibility data arriving from multiple health plans in inconsistent formats. Normalizing that into a unified analytic layer while preserving the audit trail requires treating every source format as a suspected deviation from the specification until proven otherwise.

Compliance-driven architecture is part of the role's design discipline. A recent example: a client-side Stars rating predictor where the cut-point projection runs entirely in the analyst's browser, so member-level data never transits off the machine. The browser-side compute sidesteps the data-transit review a server-side version would trigger; a side benefit has been a faster iteration loop.

pandas · scipy · numpy · dbt · SQL · Python

More detail

Across five years I led cross-functional teams to implement analytics, build measurement for clinical and operational work, and deliver sales analytics, often as the only engineer, working alongside analysts and clinical and commercial partners.

Published customer outcomes from the platform: Community Health Network closed over 373,000 care gaps in six months. St. Joseph Heritage Healthcare scaled refill processing to 37,000 monthly requests with turnaround dropping from 72 hours to 12. Valley Medical Group reduced provider-bound refill requests by 60%.

Data governance came down to one question: does a medication adherence rate mean the same thing across Epic, Cerner, athenahealth, and Veradigm? The ELT pipeline I inherited ran for multiple days and produced outputs that were difficult to trace to their source. The redesign was an auditability and governance decision, not a performance one, though it still cut weekly load latency by 24+ hours.Medallion architecture: bronze for raw ingestion, silver for documented business logic, gold for analytic output. Every transformation has a name, a location, and a test. Vendor-specific logic was parameterized at the silver tier, so one gold-level measure spec resolved across all four EHRs.

HIPAA compliance was an architectural constraint throughout. Not a certification, but a set of requirements that shaped every decision about data storage, access control, and transmission across a platform handling protected health information at scale.
