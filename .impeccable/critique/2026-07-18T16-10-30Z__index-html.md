## Anti-Patterns Verdict: PASS

LLM assessment: not AI-generated, and not close. The page trips two named reflexes on a checklist (cream `--paper` token; the editorial-typographic lane) but survives because the lane is cited, not borrowed — self-hosted ETBook, real sidenotes in a real margin, a committed rationale doc. The craft floor is the evidence: ten hand-coordinated SVGs with computed transforms, a native mobile career-arc redesign, transparent hit rects for WCAG 2.5.8, a palette-adapter contract.

The real weakness is not slop but borrowed identity. Nobody says "AI made this"; anyone design-literate says "that's Tufte CSS" in one second. The figures are Zaher's; the chrome is inherited.

Deterministic scan: 3 findings, **0 true positives**. All three are tool artifacts:
- `side-tab` L420 — `isNeutralBorderColor()` cannot resolve CSS custom properties; it captures the literal string "var" and fires. `--rule` (#d0d0c8 / #2a2f36) would have been exempted if resolved.
- `layout-transition` L1491 — substring artifact. Source is `stroke-width`, not `width`; `\b` matches at the hyphen. Emitted snippet misrepresents source.
- `numbered-section-markers` — matched ISO dates, a DOI, and CSS section comments. The actual project numbering is CSS-counter generated and invisible to a text scan.

Visual overlays: none. No browser automation exposed; live-server and injection deliberately not attempted.

## Overall Impression

A beautifully made document that is a mediocre interface — which is precisely the trade Tufte CSS makes. The hero is outstanding and arrives at second two. Everything after it declines monotonically, ending on an eleven-year-old Six Sigma Yellow Belt. The biggest opportunity is not visual; it is deciding whether this page is built for the reader who has an hour or the one who has twenty seconds.

## What's Working

1. **The career arc as hero figure.** Answers "what is this person?" before a sentence is read, and doubles as navigation. Every band is a keyboard-reachable link with a title, hit rect, and inline cascade index. The mobile version is a native redesign, not a scaled copy.
2. **Calibrated-claim discipline as a trust instrument.** "A tested proposal, not a deployed customer-analytics component." "One of the platform's published outcomes." "~10 / 100+ users." Every voluntarily scoped claim buys credibility for the ones left big.
3. **The palette-adapter contract.** The reason ten bespoke figures could accumulate without dark-mode maintenance collapsing. Infrastructure showing up as visible quality.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Thirteen phantom tab stops on desktop.** `label.margin-toggle:not(.sidenote-number)` is `display:none` above 760px (L392), but the paired checkbox stays sr-only-and-focusable (L361) with its focus ring projected onto that hidden label (L372). An outline cannot paint on a non-rendered box. Keyboard users tab into 13 invisible controls with no indication of where focus went. The tab-order preservation was a deliberate a11y decision, undone by the desktop hide on the other half of the pair. Fix: remove the checkbox from the tab order above 760px, where it has no function.

**[P1] The subtitle occupies the best line and does no work.** L1650 is a category label restating the title and meta description. This is the reassurance gap at the high-stakes moment. NOTE: locked token per CLAUDE.md §Hero — flagged, not proposed.

**[P1] Twelve disclosure regions have crossed into hidden content.** 6 of 8 posts and 4 of 6 projects invisible by default behind two-word summaries. The projects index was designed as a visible small-multiples grid; small multiples you cannot see are a list you did not write. Also: `.index-label` CSS (L735-742) is dead — zero markup uses it — and CLAUDE.md §Writing section update rule still documents it. Code and contract disagree.

**[P2] Nine nav items, no orientation, faintest type on the page.** Covers 9 of 13 sections with no stated rule. Small caps at 17px reduce effective x-height below what the 5.5:1 ratio implies.

**[P2] The page ends on its weakest content.** Peak-end rule. Last substantive impression of a fifteen-year Stars career is a 2015 Yellow Belt.
