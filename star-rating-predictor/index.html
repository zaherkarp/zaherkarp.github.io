The Stars Cliff Simulator

CMS assigns each Medicare Advantage contract a Star Rating from 1 to 5, derived from 42 measures across 9 domains. One threshold in that whole system matters more than any other. At 4.0★, a plan qualifies for Quality Bonus Payments, a 5% increase to its CMS benchmark. Below 4.0★, nothing. For a 200,000-member plan, that single tenth-of-a-star cliff is worth $50–80 million a year.

This simulator is built around that one number. An ordinal logistic regression, calibrated to CMS 2025 weights and the MA-PD distribution, collapses 42 measures into four high-leverage inputs. As you move the sliders, the readouts focus on the question an analyst actually asks: what is the probability my plan clears the 4.0★ QBP cliff, and how far are we from it right now? For the full derivation, see the methodology post.
