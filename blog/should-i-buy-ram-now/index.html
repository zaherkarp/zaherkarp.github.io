Should I Buy RAM Now?

July 5, 2026 43 min read
  • hardware
  • forecasting
  • statistics
  • ddr5

My machine runs one G.Skill Trident Z5 Neo kit — 64GB (2×32) of DDR5-6000 CL30 — and I want more memory. This year a DDR5 shortage dragged that exact kit from a boring ~$210 up to about $955, and a third-party reseller listed it near $1,140.

So I pulled the real price history from Keepa and asked a narrower question than "what will it cost": buy now, or wait — framed as the expected savings from waiting, with the uncertainty kept honest. Two facts dominate. The kit sat near $210 for two years, so today's ~$955 is roughly 4.6× its own norm and near an all-time high. And the recent plateau is flat: there is no downward trend to ride.

I made the forecast earn its uncertainty rather than assert it. It fits only the post-shortage regime, selects its smoothing parameters by walk-forward cross-validation, calibrates its intervals to real out-of-sample errors (conformal prediction), and benchmarks against a plain random walk. The result is deflating in the right way: on this plateau the model barely edges a random walk, and the calibrated 80% band for the 30-day price is only about ±$20. "Wait for it to drop" is a hope, not a forecast.

Either way there are two upgrade paths: a second identical kit toward 128GB (though four dual-rank DIMMs usually will not hold EXPO 6000 on AM5), or a single 2×64 kit that keeps the rated speed and frees the old kit for resale.
